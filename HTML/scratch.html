  1. History
    • Originated in Switzerland in the late 18th century
    • The modern version of Sudoku emerged in Indiana, USA in the late 1970s
    • Gained popularity in Japan in the 1980s
    • Exploded in popularity in the early 2000s
  2. Rules
    • A 9x9 grid, divided into nine 3x3 subgrids called "regions."
    • Fill in the grid so that each row, each column, and each region contains the numbers 1 through 9 with no repetition.
    • A partially completed puzzle will have some numbers already filled in, and the fewer numbers are given, the more difficult it is.
  3. Techniques
    • Look for rows, columns, or regions with the fewest empty cells.
    • Identify cells with only one possible number remaining
    • Fill in the cell with the remaining number
  4. Benefits
    • Enhances Cognitive Skills
    • Stress Reduction
    • Improves Concentration
